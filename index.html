The Problem

The sea urchins are consuming too much of the kelp forests and destroying the ecosystem. Uni can live for days or even weeks without needing to eat, however, they reproduce in mass and they have taken over the ocean floor, creating what's known as a “purple carpet”. Since 2014, 95 percent of the kelp have vanished across a large part of Northern California, most of it bull kelp.

Why are sea urchins bad for the Kelp Forests? Kelp forests provide a crucial ecosystem for a broad range of other marine life and animals, so their demise threatens the ecology across the entire stretch of California coast. The kelp's abrupt decline is being driven by warming waters, and it's a case of how climate change is helping push already-stressed ecosystems over the edge. The uni eat at the base of the kelp stems which inhabit the many sea creatures that lay their eggs in the kelp leaves. Without kelp forests, fish and other predators can't reproduce to control the uni population and these creatures can't maintain their own population.

Solutions

One of the most effective solutions is to harvest and consume sea urchins. By reducing their numbers, we can help kelp forests recover.

Learn how you can participate in sea urchin harvesting events, try delicious recipes, and support marine conservation efforts.

Fishing

What are the solutions to this problem?

The main solution for the uni overgrowth is actually just eating them. Although a solution can be introducing predators, raising awareness about sea urchins and encouraging locals in the area to harvest uni themselves would help reduce the uni numbers significantly. Donating money to organisations like Giant Giant Kelp Restoration Project (g2kr) and supporting Divers from Reef Check California can involve those who don't want to or can't participate with uni harvesting.

Take Action

Eating the uni is the best way to regulate the overpopulation of uni on the south coast of California. Tell your friends and family about Purple Sea Urchin and help save the kelp forests. Visit local restaurants and check out uni events near you.

Fishing for uni is easy and cost efficient. Grab a bucket and some gloves and head down to your local California coastline. Check online for low tide and limits per person. In California, it is 40 uni per person with a fishing license.

To the wonderful divers that catch and deliver fresh uni to local restaurants: thank you for cleaning up the ocean floor and helping with the population overgrowth of uni. Please consider donating to local diving centers to support their efforts.
